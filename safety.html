Child Safety Standards

Last updated: February 24, 2026

Climby is committed to providing a safe environment for all users, including minors. We have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or any form of child exploitation on our platform. This document outlines our standards, policies, and procedures for protecting minors.

Minimum Age Requirement

Climby requires all users to be at least 13 years of age to create an account and use the platform. Users between the ages of 13 and 17 are considered minors and are subject to additional protections as outlined below.

Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following content and conduct involving minors is strictly prohibited on Climby:

Any form of child sexual abuse material (CSAM), including images, videos, or text. Any content that sexualizes, exploits, or endangers minors. Grooming behavior or attempts to establish inappropriate contact with minors. Sharing personal information of minors without parental consent. Bullying, harassment, or intimidation directed at minors. Any content that promotes or glorifies harm to minors.

Detection and Prevention

Climby employs the following measures to detect and prevent child exploitation:

All user-generated content is subject to review. Users can report inappropriate content or behavior directly within the app using the built-in reporting feature. Mountains (content groups) can be restricted to friends-only, limiting exposure to unknown users. We actively monitor reports related to child safety and prioritize their review.

Reporting Mechanisms

Users can report content or behavior that may endanger minors through the following channels:

In-app reporting: Users can report any content or user profile directly within the app by tapping the report button. Email: Users can send reports to climbysocial@gmail.com with the subject line "Child Safety Report".

All child safety reports are treated with the highest priority and are reviewed promptly.

Response and Enforcement

When we identify or receive a report of content or conduct that violates our child safety standards, we take the following actions:

Immediate removal of the violating content. Suspension or permanent ban of the offending account. Preservation of evidence for law enforcement purposes. Reporting to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and other relevant authorities as required by applicable law. Cooperation with law enforcement agencies investigating child exploitation.

Cooperation with Authorities

Climby cooperates fully with law enforcement agencies and relevant authorities in the investigation of child exploitation. We comply with all applicable laws regarding the reporting of CSAM and child exploitation, including reporting to NCMEC and regional and national authorities.
